★ Feature Showcase · v2 ★

Vintage idioms for data.

Five new ways to display the league's actual numbers — borrowed from old almanacs, racing forms, ledgers, and timetables — plus the spine and ribbon from the first round.

№ I The Spine.

Sticky vertical book-spine rail to your left, with the league name printed sideways and scroll-tracked dots. You flagged this as cool but heavy for every page — the recommendation would be home page only, and have sub-pages (Standings, Managers, Seasons, etc.) drop back to the normal masthead. The spine becomes a signature element of the "entrance" rather than something visitors have to tolerate everywhere.

← Always visible on this demo. Scroll and watch the dots track.
№ II Bookmark Ribbon.

Cream silk with a deep-gold ✦ at the head — closer to a real silk page-marker than a flag. Sticky top-right. Click to bookmark the page (and snap to the top); click again to release. The bookmarked state is visually distinct — the ribbon extends noticeably longer with a soft gold halo and the ✦ swells slightly, all with a bouncy easing so it feels like a ribbon being pulled into place. Try clicking it.

↗ Top right of the viewport. Click to return.
№ III The Form Sheet.

Standings rendered as an F1 timing tower — positions on the left with up/down arrows from last week, then manager, then a five-game form string (W/L pills), points, gap from leader, and the week's swing.

On previous-season data: we'd render it from the current season only. Early in the year the form string would have empty slots (Week 1 = four empty + one pill, Week 2 = three empty + two, etc.) until five games are on file. Cleaner than mixing seasons, and the empty-pill effect itself reads as "season just begun."

№ IV The Mileage Matrix.

A vintage road atlas's distance-between-cities grid, but the cells hold head-to-head records between every pair of managers. Lower triangle only (the matrix is symmetric); diagonal cells get a hyphen. Green tint = dominant matchup, rust tint = losing matchup, neutral = even-ish. Renders on cream paper like a real atlas.

→ Live Season page candidate. Too wide and dense for the home page — but it'd be a strong addition to the live hub alongside Pick'ems, Power Rankings, etc., where readers come to dig.

№ V The Timetable.

Upcoming matchups rendered as a railway departures board — train number, departure time (game kickoff), service ("Slingers ─→─ Bench Mob"), platform (matchup number in Roman), and live status with a pulsing dot for BOARDING. Reads at a glance.

→ Live Season page candidate. Belongs on the live hub where the week's schedule actually matters — sits well next to Pick'ems and the power-rank widgets. Too wide for the home page's tight rhythm.

№ VI The Day Book.

A vintage ledger / day book of recent league activity. Lined paper, mono dates running down the rule margin, italic serif prose for each entry, single-letter W/L mark on the right in a small square. Could show the latest 6–10 events: results, milestones, trades, roster moves. Reads like a Victorian shopkeeper's account book.

№ VII The Almanac Calendar.

A real month-grid calendar in the almanac's cream-and-rust palette, with each day flagged for what happened or what's coming: GAME cells, MILESTONE cells, TX (trades) cells. Today gets a thick rust border so you can find it. Could be a fixed widget on the home page, advancing month by month.

№ VIII The Trackboard.

For the home page: a narrow vertical tracker with a tabbed view across the top — Hot, Cold, Top PF, Watch. Same monospace clean look as the Form Sheet, sized around 360px so it slots into a sidebar (or stacks beside the Editor's Note). Each tab swaps to a different lens on what's happening this season — try clicking them. The Watch tab uses a different row format with a progress bar showing how close a manager is to setting/breaking a record.

Want to see it sticky on the right? Hit Preview pinned-right below — it'll detach and follow you as you scroll, so you can judge whether that's the right placement. Click again to release.
